# PRODUCT.md — Athlone GAA

## Register

brand

## Product purpose

The public face of Athlone GAA club: fixtures, results, news, teams, membership. The site must make the town proud and make a teenager want to show it to their mates after training. It is a supporters' surface first, an information surface second.

## Users

- Supporters checking Sunday's throw-in time on a phone, walking to the pitch in grey Irish daylight.
- Parents finding summer camp and membership details.
- Young players and their friends: the site should feel like the county final, not a council newsletter.

## Brand voice

Grass-stained, thunderous, parish-proud. The GAA is amateur sport played with professional intensity: volunteers, floodlit winter training, generations of the same families on the same pitch since 1905. The voice is a matchday poster and a stadium PA, never a corporate brochure.

Three physical-object references: a county-final match programme on cheap cream paper; a hand-painted scoreboard with sliding numbers; chalk lines freshly marked on wet grass.

## Aesthetic lane

Matchday poster: Nike "Nothing Beats a Londoner" energy crossed with a vintage GAA programme. Enormous condensed caps, kinetic scoreboards, duotone action photography, chalk-line motifs. NOT editorial-magazine, NOT tech-minimal, NOT glassmorphism.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages (hero metric cards, identical icon-card grids).
- Council/parish-newsletter blandness.
- Glass pills and blurred cards (the previous design; retired).
- Anything a visitor could call "AI made that".

## Non-negotiables

- The club crest (`/assets/imgImage232.png`) is kept everywhere; it is the only surviving element of the old design.
- Copy never uses em dashes.
- Motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.
